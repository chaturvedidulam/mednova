# NIH Diabetes Mellitus Criteria
Source: National Institutes of Health (NIH)
Citation: [NIH Diabetes Indicators 2025, page 4]

Diagnostic criteria for Diabetes Mellitus and Pre-diabetes based on Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG) and HbA1c:
- Normal: FPG < 100 mg/dL (5.6 mmol/L) and HbA1c < 5.7%. [NIH Diabetes Indicators 2025, page 4]
- Pre-diabetes: FPG 100-125 mg/dL (5.6-6.9 mmol/L) or HbA1c 5.7% to 6.4%. [NIH Diabetes Indicators 2025, page 5]
- Diabetes Mellitus: FPG >= 126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L) or HbA1c >= 6.5%. [NIH Diabetes Indicators 2025, page 5]
- Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT) 2-hour glucose: Normal < 140 mg/dL, Pre-diabetes 140-199 mg/dL, Diabetes >= 200 mg/dL. [NIH Diabetes Indicators 2025, page 6]
- Random Plasma Glucose: >= 200 mg/dL in a patient with symptoms of hyperglycemia (polyuria, polydipsia, unexplained weight loss). [NIH Diabetes Indicators 2025, page 7]

Recommendations:
- For pre-diabetes, initiate intensive lifestyle modifications (such as 150 minutes per week of moderate-intensity physical activity and weight loss targeting 7% body weight). Consider metformin therapy, especially for patients under age 60, BMI >= 35, or history of gestational diabetes. [NIH Diabetes Indicators 2025, page 10]
- For diagnosed diabetes, primary pharmacotherapy includes metformin unless contraindicated. Regular self-monitoring of blood glucose and target HbA1c < 7.0% are standard goals. [NIH Diabetes Indicators 2025, page 12]
