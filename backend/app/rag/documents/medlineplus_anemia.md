# MedlinePlus Anemia Guide
Source: MedlinePlus (National Library of Medicine)
Citation: [MedlinePlus NLM Anemia Overview 2026]

Anemia occurs when the blood has a lower-than-normal amount of red blood cells or hemoglobin.
Normal Hemoglobin (Hb) levels are defined as:
- Adult males: 13.8 to 17.2 g/dL. [MedlinePlus NLM Anemia Overview 2026]
- Adult females: 12.1 to 15.1 g/dL. [MedlinePlus NLM Anemia Overview 2026]

Fermoglobin or ferritin levels below the standard range indicate iron-deficiency anemia.
Symptoms of anemia include extreme fatigue, weakness, pale skin, cold hands and feet, dizziness, headache, brittle nails, and unusual cravings for non-nutritive substances (pica).

Recommendations:
- For iron-deficiency anemia, increase dietary intake of iron-rich foods, including red meat, poultry, seafood, beans, dark green leafy vegetables (spinach), and iron-fortified cereals. [MedlinePlus NLM Anemia Overview 2026]
- Take vitamin C (ascorbic acid) or drink citrus juice alongside iron-rich foods or iron supplements to significantly enhance iron absorption in the gut. [MedlinePlus NLM Anemia Overview 2026]
- Oral iron supplements (such as ferrous sulfate) should be taken as prescribed under clinician supervision. [MedlinePlus NLM Anemia Overview 2026]
