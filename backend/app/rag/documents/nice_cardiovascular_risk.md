# NICE Cardiovascular Risk Assessment (CG181)
Source: National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE)
Citation: [NICE Guideline CG181, 2025 Cardiovascular Prevention]

Lipid level recommendations for primary and secondary cardiovascular risk assessment:
- Total cholesterol: < 5.0 mmol/L (approx. 190 mg/dL) for healthy adults. [NICE Guideline CG181, 2025 Cardiovascular Prevention]
- LDL cholesterol ("bad" cholesterol): < 3.0 mmol/L (approx. 115 mg/dL) for healthy adults, and < 2.0 mmol/L (approx. 80 mg/dL) for high-risk patients. [NICE Guideline CG181, 2025 Cardiovascular Prevention]
- HDL cholesterol ("good" cholesterol): > 1.0 mmol/L (approx. 40 mg/dL) for men, and > 1.2 mmol/L (approx. 45 mg/dL) for women. [NICE Guideline CG181, 2025 Cardiovascular Prevention]
- Triglycerides: < 1.7 mmol/L fasting. [NICE Guideline CG181, 2025 Cardiovascular Prevention]

Recommendations:
- Offer atorvastatin 20mg daily for primary prevention of cardiovascular disease to people with a 10% or greater 10-year risk of developing cardiovascular disease, calculated using the QRISK3 tool. [NICE Guideline CG181, 2025 Cardiovascular Prevention]
- Offer atorvastatin 80mg daily for secondary prevention of cardiovascular disease to all people with established cardiovascular disease (ischaemic heart disease, stroke, or peripheral arterial disease). [NICE Guideline CG181, 2025 Cardiovascular Prevention]
- Direct lifestyle adjustments should include smoking cessation, reduction in saturated fat intake, replacement of saturated fats with monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats, and at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity weekly. [NICE Guideline CG181, 2025 Cardiovascular Prevention]
