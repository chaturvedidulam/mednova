# CDC Influenza and Respiratory Infection Protocol
Source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
Citation: [CDC Respiratory Guidance 2026, page 12]

Influenza and common respiratory infections present with symptoms including sudden onset of fever, cough, chest pain, sore throat, fatigue, and muscle aches.

Emergency Warning Signs (Seek Immediate Medical Care):
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath.
- Persistent pain or pressure in the chest.
- New confusion or cognitive impairment.
- Inability to wake or stay awake.
- Pale, gray, or blue skin, lips, or nail beds (cyanosis). [CDC Respiratory Guidance 2026, page 15]

Recommendations:
- Annual influenza vaccination is recommended for everyone aged 6 months and older. [CDC Respiratory Guidance 2026, page 3]
- Antiviral treatment (e.g., oseltamivir / Tamiflu) should be initiated as soon as possible for patients with confirmed or suspected influenza who are hospitalized, have severe progressive illness, or are at high risk of complications (e.g., elderly, chronic heart or lung disease, diabetes). [CDC Respiratory Guidance 2026, page 12]
- Support symptom management with rest, hydration, and antipyretics (acetaminophen/ibuprofen) for non-emergency viral cases. [CDC Respiratory Guidance 2026, page 16]
