# WHO Guidelines on Hypertension Management (2025)
Source: World Health Organization (WHO)
Citation: [WHO Hypertension Guidelines 2025, Section 3.1]

Hypertension is diagnosed when systolic blood pressure (SBP) is >= 140 mmHg and/or diastolic blood pressure (DBP) is >= 90 mmHg.
- Normal blood pressure: SBP < 120 mmHg and DBP < 80 mmHg.
- Elevated blood pressure: SBP 120-129 mmHg and DBP < 80 mmHg.
- Stage 1 Hypertension: SBP 130-139 mmHg or DBP 80-89 mmHg.
- Stage 2 Hypertension: SBP >= 140 mmHg or DBP >= 90 mmHg.

Recommendations:
- For patients with SBP >= 140 mmHg or DBP >= 90 mmHg (Stage 2 Hypertension), pharmacological treatment (ACE inhibitors, ARBs, calcium channel blockers, or thiazide diuretics) is recommended immediately alongside lifestyle modifications. [WHO Hypertension Guidelines 2025, Section 3.1]
- For patients with SBP 130-139 mmHg or DBP 80-89 mmHg (Stage 1 Hypertension) who have cardiovascular disease or high risk, pharmacological treatment should be initiated. [WHO Hypertension Guidelines 2025, Section 3.4]
